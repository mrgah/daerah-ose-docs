A PbtA amuse-bouche

In-game, most of the time we play w/OSE rules and procedures

But there are also some times and places we use PbtA die rolls to: - resolve game mechanics - but also to allow players authorship

Some ideas for PbtA procedures

  • roll to connect w/another PC
    • success means a bond that can be spent for advantage
    • failure means you fail to connect, in an interesting way
  • roll to explore/do stuff in downtime location/do downtime activities (advance, e.g.)
    • successes mean you get what you want
    • 9 and lower mean interesting complications that you get to co-author
  • roll to return to the surface if you're down below at session end
    • 7-9 means an encounter
    • 6- means you fail to return to the surface

PbtA hexcrawling

  • fast v. slow travel
    • hidden things can (only) be found on a roll of 10-12
    • slow travel: 2d6+some stat mod, one roll per hex (or whatever)
    • fast travel: straight 2d6, so lower possibility of finding hidden things
    • under the hood, 9- rolls may require rolls on encounter tables, etc
    • if you've traveled a certain way before, chance of encounter drops (encounters on 6- rather than 9-)

Dungeon traversal

  • dungeon traversal gets easier with a map
  • 7-9 causes encounter, but you also get 1 hold
    • can spend hold to ask question from DW's Discern Realities list
  • 10+ is a successful traversal
  • 6- means that traversal fails and you get lost, but you also get 1 hold
    • if you're attempting to return to the surface, your PC doesn't make it to the surface
  • maps confer:
    • +mod to traversal roll
    • but nat 2-3 on traversal roll w/map means damage to map
  • can use labyrinth move if needed

Languages

  • in immediate area, everyone has some familiarity with imperial common
  • may use the language tick system from Spider Queen for more abstruse languages
