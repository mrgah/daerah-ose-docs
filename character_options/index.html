Character Options

Thief

There are two options if you want to play a thief/specialist: the simple version or the slightly more complicated version.

Option 1: Thieving for the simple

Thieves don't have to keep track of all of these percentile skills that are given in OSE RAW. You just roll a d6 if you want to do something thiefy and it's plausible, you just roll a d6. If you roll high enough, you succeed; "high enough" can be decided by GM fiat, but a base class skill chance by level will look like this: | Level | Class skill chance | |-----------|-----------------------| | 1 | 1 in 6 | | 2 | 2 in 6 | | 3 | 2 in 6 | | 4 | 3 in 6 | | 5 | 3 in 6 | | 6 | 4 in 6 | | 7 | 4 in 6 | | 8 | 5 in 6 | | 9 | 5 in 6 | | 10 | 6 in 6[^1] | [^1]: roll 2d6; failure only occurs if both are 1s

Option 2: The LotFP Specialist

If you're fine with a bit more complexity and/or want to customize, the Lamentations of the Flame Princess Specialist has a nice skill system, including thiefy stuff as well as other things (Architecture, Bushcraft, Languages, Tinker, etc.). Only tweak is that Sneak Attack as written is pretty OP, so points in Sneak Attack will be capped by level: max 2 points at Level 1, 3 points at Level 5, etc.

Wizards

We're going to use the version of GLoG wizards in Skerples' v2 of the GLoG (https://coinsandscrolls.blogspot.com/2019/10/osr-glog-based-homebrew-v2-many-rats-on.html). Almost everything will be the same as in Skerples' presentation; I have thoughts about where many of the wizard schools will fit in our setting, but let me know if you want to take one of the very weird schools.

If and when a wizard gets to level 5 and beyond, we're going to follow the spell progression in B/X / OSE. I.e., a level 5 wizard will get one spell from the OSE Level 3 spell list. We'll be using the Skerples formulation of those spells where they exist (fireball), or adapting the OSE definition to the GLoG rules where they don't (see the DM for details). Expected values for some spells (fireball) are lower in GLoG than in OSE; the tradeoff is that you might get to retain your magic dice (MD) and cast the same spell multiple times, depending on how your rolls play out.

PC wizards will acquire spells automatically upon leveling up. It's an episodic open table game, so the assumption is that your PC is studying and advancing their knowledge between sessions.
